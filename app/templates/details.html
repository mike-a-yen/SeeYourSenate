Technical Details


Description of data

The U.S. Senate votes on several hundred bills each year. All of the data used by SYS is publicly available and free to download. You can download the voting record of each congressional session all the way back to the first congress. SYS keeps track of each congressional bill and each senators' voting record in order to make the most accurate predictions on which bills will become laws. Our database contains over 135,000 congressional bills and 6,500 senatorial sessions going back to the 103rd congress, which served in 1993 and 1994.

Live Updates

SYS stays up to date with the senate's hectic schedule and continuously updates each senator's voting record and keeps track of all new bills being introduced to the senate.

Prediction Model

What makes SYS's bill predictions accurate is the leverage gained by modeling each senator individually. By aggregating these individual models into one super model we are able to boost our prediction accuracy by almost 10%, making our pass/fail predictions over 80% accurate.

Each senator has a predictive model that is based on his or her own voting record. The predictions made by each model are influenced by the senator's voting record on bills with similar subject and content. For example, if a bill about Women's healthcare is introduced to the senate SYS will look through each senators' voting record on bills about women's rights, healthcare, and women's healthcare to make its predictions.
