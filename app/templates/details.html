Technical Details


Description of data

The U.S. Senate votes on several hundred bills each year. All of the data used by SYS is publicly available and free to download. You can download the voting record of each congressional session all the way back to the first congress. Most of the data used by SYS to make predictions is based off of the title, main subject, and textual content of each bill. Our database contains the voting record of each senator going back to the 103rd congress, which served in 1993 and 1994.

Model Details

What makes SYS's bill predictions accurate is the leverage gained by modeling each senator individually. By aggregating these individual models into one super model we are able to boost our prediction accuracy by almost 10%, making our pass/fail predictions over 80% accurate. The math behind this boost is the same as ensemble machine learning methods.

Each senator has a predictive model that is based on his or her own voting record. The predictions made by each model are influenced by the senator's voting record on bills with similar subject and content.
