About

SYS has built a model that predicts the probability of a bill passing through the United States senate. We model the voting behavior of each senator from the text contained inside the bill, and with this information determine the probability of the bill being passed into law. Preliminary evidence has shown that SYS can predict the outcome of each bill with 80% accuracy. That is 8 out of 10 times SYS correctly determines if the bill passes or fails.

In addition to this, we also summarize the voting behavior of individual senators, so you can get an idea about what your representative is voting for. This information can be used to see if your representative is likely to vote for bills you care about. Check out the Senator page for more details.

Preliminary Analysis

The following analysis was done by studying the voting habits of Senator Barbara Boxer (CA-D) over the last 16 years. In total, she participated in over 5,000 votes, with an approximately 50-50 split between "Yay" and "Nay" votes. By vectorizing the text in the headers of each bill, I was able to build a model that predicted Boxer's voting behavior with 70% accuracy, which is an improvement over random guessing. If I build a similar model for all other senators, retaining the 70% accuracy, I will be able to correctly predict wether or not a bill will pass the senate with an accuracy of 80%.

Large dots are "Yay" votes and smaller dots are "Nay" votes, and from this visualization you can see Boxer has a tendency to vote "Yay" for all bills in some clusters and "Nay" for bills in other clusters.

The fact that Boxer has a pattern of voting for or against bills with certain content, allows for a model to be built to predict her voting behavior.

Correct Prediction Probability

{{ stats_plot|safe }}

This model will correctly predict the "pass" or "no pass" outcome with 80% accuracy.

The voting behavior of the senate as a whole can be predicted by the voting behavior of individual senators. This representation can be more accurate than random if the individual predictive models for each senator are more accurate than 50%. This increased accuracy allows better predictions to be made on the outcomes of individual bills. Building 100 individual models that can predict the voting behavior of each senator with an accuracy of 70% or greater, will correctly determine whether or not the bill passes with an accuracy of at least 80%.

This means when fully built, this model will correctly predict the "pass" or "no pass" outcome of future laws with an accuracy of 80%.
